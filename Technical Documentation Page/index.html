Introduction
Context:

  • Half-metallic ferromagnets (HMFs) and spin gapless semiconductors (SGSs) are promising for spintronics due to high spin polarization. HMFs have a gap at the Fermi level for one spin direction, while SGSs combine HMF properties with semiconductor characteristics.

Objective:

  • Study the electrical, magnetic, and galvanomagnetic properties of Mn₂MeAl (Me = Ti, V, Cr, Mn, Fe, Co, Ni) to identify HMF and SGS states.

Experimental Methods
Sample Preparation:

  • Alloys melted in an induction furnace under purified argon and annealed at 650°C for 72 hours.

Structural Analysis:

  • X-ray diffraction and SEM with EDAX microanalysis.

  • Mn₂TiAl, Mn₂VAl, and Mn₂CrAl have L2₁ structure; others have inverse Xₐ structure.

Measurements:

  • Electrical resistivity (4.2 K to 900 K) using a four-probe method.

  • Hall effect and magnetization at 4.2 K in magnetic fields up to 100 kOe.

Results Electrical and Magnetic Properties
Electrical Resistivity:

  • High residual resistivity (242-305 µΩ·cm) and negative temperature coefficient in Mn₂TiAl, Mn₂CrAl, Mn₃Al, Mn₂FeAl, and Mn₂CoAl, suggesting SGS states.

  • Low residual resistivity (84-120 µΩ·cm) and monotonic increase in Mn₂VAl and Mn₂NiAl.

Magnetization:

  • Mn₂VAl and Mn₂CoAl show typical ferromagnetic behavior.

  • Mn₂TiAl and Mn₂NiAl exhibit complex magnetic order, possibly ferrimagnetic.

  • Mn₃Al, Mn₂CrAl, and Mn₂FeAl have near-zero total magnetic moment, indicating possible half-metallic antiferromagnetic states.

Results Hall Effect and Correlations
Hall Effect:

  • Normal (R₀) and anomalous (Rₛ) Hall coefficients observed.

  • R₀ related to Lorentz force; Rₛ linked to spin-orbit interaction and magnetic scattering.

Correlations:

  • Significant changes in resistivity, magnetization, and Hall effect with varying Me component.

  • Clear correlation between electronic transport and magnetic properties as the number of valence electrons (z) changes from 21 to 27.

Conclusions and Future Perspectives
Conclusions:

  • Mn₂MeAl alloys exhibit features indicative of HMF and SGS states.

  • Electrical resistivity, magnetization, and Hall effect vary significantly with Me component.

Future Perspectives:

  • Neutron diffraction studies to clarify magnetic order.

  • Fine-tuning composition for optimized spintronic applications.
